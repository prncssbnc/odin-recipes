Potato Mojos

Home

Potato mojos are crispy, battered, and deep-fried potato slices, often replicating the popular Shakey’s style, featuring a soft interior and seasoned crunchy exterior. They are made by soaking sliced, unpeeled potatoes in seasoned egg/milk mixtures, coating them in flour or breading mix, and frying until golden, served with creamy dips.

Ingredients

For the Potatoes:

  • Potatoes
  • Crispy fry, 30g
  • All-purpose flour, 3 tbsp
  • Eggs, beaten, 2

For the Garlic Mayo Dip:

  • Mayonnaise, 4 tbsp
  • Garlic, 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Sugar, 1 tsp
  • Calamansi, 1/2 tsp

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Cut the unpeeled potatoes into thin slices, about 1/4 inch thick.
  2. Place 1-2 cups oil (about one inch deep) in a large frying pan. Heat oil over medium heat until water sizzles across the top. While oil is heating, coat potato slices with crispy fry breading.
  3. Carefully drop potatoes into the oil right away (so the batter doesn’t get soggy) and fry for 2-3 minutes on each side, adjusting heat as necessary.
  4. Remove and drain on a paper towel lined plate. Serve with garlic mayo dip.
